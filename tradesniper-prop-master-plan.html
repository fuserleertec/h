02   Research Findings

What Actually Wins in Prop Firms

🎯
Quality Over Quantity — Max 2–3 Setups Per Day
Elite prop traders cap at 2–3 high-conviction trades daily. Overtrading is the #1 account killer — spreads and commissions compound against you, and decision fatigue degrades execution. USME's AI scoring system already filters this for you.
Critical Rule
📐
Risk 0.25–1% Per Trade, Stop at 2–3% Daily Loss
Most firms allow 5% daily drawdown, but winning traders self-impose a 2–3% personal stop. Risk per trade scales down after two consecutive red days — reduce sizing by 50%.
Risk Management
⏰
Trade Killzones Only — London Open & New York Open
ICT/SMC setups have the highest win rates during 2:00–5:00 AM EST London Open and 7:00–10:00 AM EST NY Open. These are when institutional order flow is heaviest and USME's FVGs, OBs, and BOS signals align with real institutional activity.
USME Native
📊
Minimum 1:2 RR — Never Take Below 1:1.5
A 40–45% win rate at 1:2 RR outperforms a 60% win rate at 1:1. USME's ATR-based stops and targets are built for this. The goal is not to be right — it's to extract asymmetric payouts when you are.
Math-Based Edge
🧠
Never Chase the Profit Target — Distribute Across Days
Many firms have a consistency rule where no single day can contribute more than 30–40% of total profits toward a payout. Aim for 0.5–1% per day on evaluation accounts — steady, surgical, repeatable.
Consistency Rule
📰
Avoid News Events Unless the Firm Explicitly Allows It
Trading NFP, CPI, FOMC on prop accounts has ended thousands of funded accounts. Close all positions 2 minutes before any red-folder event. Check firm rules per account — no exceptions.
Risk Warning

08   Non-Negotiables

The 7 Laws of USME Prop Trading

01
Never Risk More Than 1% Per Trade on Funded Accounts
Your edge is statistical. A single oversized position can breach daily drawdown and erase weeks of progress. USME ATR stops + 1% sizing is the formula. No exceptions.
02
Stop Trading After 2% Daily Loss. Full Stop.
Two bad trades don't become three. Shut the platform. Revenge trading inside prop firm rules is terminal — it's how funded accounts die in one session.
03
Only Trade Killzone Windows
USME signals outside London and NY Open are lower quality. Boredom trading during dead sessions manufactures losing trades on accounts that were otherwise performing perfectly.
04
Never Scale Before Proving Consistency
Adding a 2nd account before the 1st has 2+ consecutive profitable months with payouts is premature. You're not scaling an edge — you're scaling a mistake.
05
Never Hold Through News on Restricted Accounts
Check the firm's news policy before every trade. Close before any red-folder event. The few pips you might gain are not worth account termination.
06
Withdraw Profits Regularly
Leaving profits in funded accounts creates false confidence and inflated exposure. Withdraw on schedule, reinvest only into new challenges. Protect what you've earned.
07
Journal Every Trade in TraderEdge OS
Across multiple accounts, pattern recognition is impossible without a journal. TraderEdge tracks which USME setups perform best per firm, per session, per pair. That data is your second edge.
